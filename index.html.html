From 'where to bring the kids this weekend' to 'why is my teenager so quiet' — twenty one years, still figuring it out together.

Started in 2005 with three kids, no strategy, and a simple question: where should we go this weekend? Twenty one years later, the families who found this when their kids were small are still here — some of them now asking about NS. Didn't see that coming.

The Brand

Twenty one years.
One audience. Real trust.

In 2005, a dad started writing honest notes about where to bring the kids in Singapore. No PR angle. No content strategy. Just useful, real-talk posts that parents could actually act on. They bookmarked it. Shared it. Came back. Twenty one years later, cheekiemonkies reaches over 110,000 followers across platforms, with an Instagram engagement rate 19 times the industry benchmark. That kind of trust isn't bought. It's built post by post, over two decades.

01
2005 – 2018
Three Kids, No Clue, One Blog

Honest reviews. Practical details. No PR gloss. cheekiemonkies became the resource Singapore parents actually used — bookmarked, shared in group chats, returned to every school holiday. The content worked because it respected the reader: a busy parent who needed a straight answer, not a sponsored caption. That reputation for reliability became the platform's most durable asset.

02
2018 – Now
Saying the Quiet Parts Out Loud

As the audience's children grew, so did the content. O-Level stress. NS enlistment mornings. The teenager who'd gone quiet at dinner. Posts on these moments travelled further and faster than anything before — shared into WhatsApp threads, forwarded between spouses, saved for conversations parents hadn't had yet. The audience didn't just read. They acted. That shift from reach to resonance is what separates cheekiemonkies from every other family platform in Singapore.

03
Now →
When The House Goes Quiet

Today cheekiemonkies covers the whole arc of a Singapore family — weekend outings, O-Level chaos, NS weekends, and the chapter no other parenting platform reaches: the empty nest, and what comes after. No other local family platform has stayed with the same audience through every one of these stages. That continuity is the authority. And authority is what makes a partner's message land.

The Founder

There's a real person
behind all of this.

I didn't come from media. What I brought instead was something harder to manufacture: lived experience, and an audience that knew the difference. I'm a Singapore dad who started writing about family life because I was in the middle of it — and I never stopped.

The content started with family outings — honest, practical write-ups that parents could trust. Over time it grew into something larger: a platform that Singapore families return to at every stage, from toddler weekends to O-Level evenings to NS mornings. That breadth of reach, sustained over twenty one years, is what makes cheekiemonkies different.

The audience grew because the content earned it — honest, consistent, and always in service of the reader rather than the brand. That's the same editorial standard that governs every partnership today.

Twenty one years of consistent publishing built something beyond reach. Today I sit on three government advisory councils — MOE COMPASS, MSF Families for Life, and MINDEF ACCORD. I'm a Certified Mental Wellness Coach, a published author, and a regular voice in national media on parenting, youth development, and family resilience. That institutional credibility transfers directly to every partnership — and it's why brands in education, healthcare, and government choose cheekiemonkies for their most sensitive briefs.
